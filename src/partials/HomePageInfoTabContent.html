Linlithgow Palace (EH49 7AL) is in the town centre. Low Port Centre (EH49 7HZ) is on Blackness Road on the east side of the town, opposite Tesco.

by Road

Junction 3 or 4 from M9 motorway There is limited parking at the Palace itself, with some spaces reserved for Blue Badge holders. There is also plenty of parking a short walk away - please consider using this. At the Low Port itself, parking is even more limited, but there is a large car park over the road near Teso.

by Rail

Linlithgow is on the Edinburgh-Glasgow railway line. The station is about 0.5km from either venue.

Linlithgow - A Great Visit

Dancing

We usually have a piper to welcome you to the Palace. Sometimes these are professional musicians, sometimes they are talented students (the Scotish Conservatoire offers degreees in piping).

Each night we have a high quality live Scottish band, chosen for their ability to make dancing most enjoyable.

There is a wide range of dances, Scottish and international. Our experienced caller gives instruction for them all, before and during the dance.
Each night we include a small number of more difficult Scottish Country dances, danced at the same time, to the same music, as an easier one. Instructions for the harder dance are given before the dance but not during it.

The Palace Courtyard is open air with stone paving, and can be quite cool.

The Low Port Centre is a large hall with a sprung floor, and usually gets very warm!

Venue Details

Which Venue & Where? Details?

Whenever possible, we will dance in the Palace. However, as an open-air venue, there will be times when the weather will not be suitable. On these occasions we will use our alternative indoors venue, for the sake of the band as well as the dancers.
A decision is made as late as possible (up to 6.00pm). If there is any doubt, the dancing will be inside, at the Low Port Centre, Blackness Road opposite Tesco to the East side of town.

Finish

The programme usually takes us up to about 10.00 p.m. in the Palace. It may also get a bit chilly and dark by this time, especially in the later weeks - we do have lighting, but heating is not feasible.

We usually finish about 10.30 p.m. in Low Port Centre.

Seating

There is limited availability of seating at both venues.

Wheelchair access

Palace - one small step

Low Port Centre - ramps and lift.

At both venues, space is reserved for wheelchairs.

Toilets

The Palace has limited accommodation, but has a disabled one.

There are also toilets in the Burgh Halls in the Kirkgate on the approach to the Palace.

The Low Port has ample toilets, including some with disabled access.
