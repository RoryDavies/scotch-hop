UPDATED PROGRAMME FOR 2016

Music and Dancing

We usually have a piper to welcome you to the Palace. Sometimes these are professional musicians, sometimes they are talented students (the Scottish Conservatoire offers degrees in piping).

Each night we have a high quality live Scottish band, chosen for their ability to make dancing most enjoyable.

There is a wide range of dances, Scottish and international. Our experienced caller gives instruction for them all, before and during the dance.
Each night we include a small number of more difficult Scottish Country dances, danced at the same time, to the same music, as an easier one. Instructions for the harder dance are given before the dance but not during it.

The programmes shown below allow you to research the dances beforehand if you prefer. The programmes are subject to change according to conditions on the night.

The Palace Courtyard is open air with stone paving, and can be quite cool.

The Low Port Centre is a large hall with a sprung floor, and usually gets very warm!
