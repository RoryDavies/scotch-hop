Venue Details

Whenever possible, we will dance in the Palace. However, as an open-air venue, there will be times when the weather will not be suitable. On these occasions we will dance indoors at the Low Port, for the sake of the band as well as the dancers and spectators.

A decision is made as late as possible (up to 6.00pm) and will be posted on twitter (see the Latest tab). We will also have people or signs at the Palace directing you to the Low Port.

Details of the Palace

Seating

There is limited availability of seating. Like the dancing, it is outdoors, and it can get chilly despite the shelter provided by the Palace walls. So we recommend warm clothing or a blanket, especially if you may be watching more than dancing.

Wheelchair access

There is a ramp at the entrance, and some parts of the surface are uneven, but it is generally accessible to wheelchairs. Some space is reserved for wheelchairs.

Toilets

There is one toilet in the Palace, reserved for the disabled. This cannot be used during tea breaks.

There are ample toilets in the Burgh Halls in the Kirkgate on the approach to the Palace.

Details of the Low Port

The Low Port Centre is on Blackness Road opposite Tesco to the East side of town.

Entry is on the ground floor, dancing is in a large hall on the first floor.

Seating

There is limited availability of seating. The venue can get very warm, espeially for dancers.

Wheelchair access

There are ramps and a lift, giving good access.

Some space is reserved for wheelchairs.

Toilets

There are plenty of toilets, with facilities for the disabled. Toilets are on the ground floor, and access by the lift may be inaccessible during the tea break.
